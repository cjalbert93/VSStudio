Why?

"To tell you the truth, I originally made these for myself. As a visual and hands-on learner, I struggled through more than two decades of formal education. Throughout this process I learned that the method (for example hearing, seeing, doing) which a person learns information will determine how that information is physically stored inside the human and that real 3D objects have this incredibly old and powerful capacity to be learned and understood.

I ended up teaching myself 90% of the information through books and online media as I pursued my degree. What I needed were 3D objects which I could see and play with which I frantically built as I survived being an artist studying mechanical engineering.

Ultimately I discovered that the world lacks an interactive, visual library, so I got busy building one. This website (and soon to be app) will only ever reach its potential when it opens itself up to the online community and so I am proud to welcome you early explorers to my humble endeavor. I am grateful for each and every one of you."

-Colin "the rabbit"

What?

TREEMATRIX is a library of dynamic visual knowledge and tools built within a game system framework. The tools were developed using a leveling system where zero is the base model and the following models build levels of complexity atop of one another.

These tools explore the underlying structure of reality, the complexity of the human mind/body and illuminate a direct path for growth and development.

TREEMATRIX is composed of a two dimensional grid-like matrix which holds and connects a series of three dimensional objects.

Fixed to the top left of the site is your account information and your masteries. Those are your trophies for completing the various tool-tree levels. The icons state your current level and will appear green when every level has a verified status, and clear when the tool-tree remains unexplored. TREEMATRIX works on an honor system, you can click the verify-bell inside any tool-tree whenever you feel you have absorbed the information and/or completed the challenges.

Below you will find the lowest level model, The Lv. 0 Matrix and below that, a toolbar to navigate the various tool-trees. We strongly urge you to take your time and enjoy the wonder that is learning.
